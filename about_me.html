About me

Academic

Currently I am a DPhil student at the University of Oxford, based in the Oxford Centre for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (OCIAM), part of the Mathematical Institute, where I am supervised by Mason Porter. Previously I studied at University College London for a MSci in Mathematics with Economics, which I completed with a First in 2011. I finished school with a European Baccalaureate from the European School Brussels III in 2007. Before moving to Brussels in 2003, I went to primary and secondary school in Germany.

Personal

I was born in Brussels in 1989, and spent about half my childhood there, moving to Germany and then back again twice. While I am officially German, I actually feel more at home in Belgium and the UK most of the time. I picked up a fair amount of French while living in Brussels, though my German and English are definitely a lot better.

When I am not looking at networks, I like to jam on my saxophone or go climbing. I also like mountain-biking, though this has been confined to the summer holidays by the lack of proper mountains near any of the cities I have lived in for the past few years.
